How I grew up behind the Great Firewall.

Like Neil in the "Dead Poets Society", I struggled against traditional conservative education for most of my life.
Born to a conservative Chinese family, my family wanted me to be a bureaucrat. I was sent to an elite boarding school, with my freedom torn from me. Surrounded by soulless schoolmates, we repeated our studies everyday, until the infamous China college entrance exams six years later. All through school, I was bullied relentlessly because of my quiet persona and independent mentality. These experiences tempered my soul against the brutual study drills. I channeled my frustrations to study harder, aspiring to be a teacher and uplift children suffering within the Chinese education system.
In 2022, I successfully finished the college entrance examination.
My family almost forced me to apply for the Chinese language major, with the easiest route to the civil service exam. I resisted and chose the education major, still hoping to become a teacher. But reality was merciless in shattering my naivety. We were only taught to improve students' scores and exam skills. After a year, I suddenly remembered the "Dead Poets Society" on one particularly bleak night: I realized I could never become a good educator in China.I sought to change my major as a 2nd year. However, transferring to another college would demote me by a year! Depressed and desperate, the only option left was the translation major, which also offered more spare time. I thought this was beneficial, freeing up more time for myself to fight for a better future.
Where should l go? Should l compromise with fate and become a civil servant in this twisted world? How can I find another way? What kind of job do I love? Despite eight years of schooling, the future only looked bleaker and more grim. Lost in the fog, surrounded by the soulless, and unable to see clearly ahead... I only knew that studying liberal arts in China's current economic situation would result in being jobless, and my family will surely seize me home to be a government bureaucrat.
Somehow, I stumbled upon psychometric tests, which indicated I was suitable for UX design. Unfortunately, no university in China offered this major, so I had planned to study abroad.
My family agreed to my request at first, but later they thought it would be a huge waste of their money due to the bleak economy and their presumption that I would eventually return home to become a civil servant; to be their obedient doll. As per tradition, they would rather save the money to bribe officials to find a good department for me. I was deeply hurt. Tears streamed down my face and a sense of melancholy overwhelmed me. In my sleepless nights,I asked my self many times:Am I a bird unable to seek freedom in their cage?
On my birthday, I chose to cheer myself up. Because of China's firewall, access to information is very limited. I bought a VPN and learned that many people changed jobs through self-study. l was inspired by these brave and self-disciplined people. I watched videos about self-learning and made plans. Eventually, I planned to study on Coursera.
Despite my university's load at 26 classes a week, I still found time and energy to study in the library at night after class every day. l learned how to wireframe. I learned Figma. l strove hard from the initial simple wireframe to the final responsive web design. Finally, I successfully obtained the Google UX certificate. At that very moment of completion, I was ecstatic for myself; self-study had become my solace in the darkness. I now believe I can change my ill-fated life. I am increasingly happier on my life journey. In 2025, I went to Malaysia to relax and get in touch with new cultures. At that time, I realised fate did not limit me; it was tradition and conservative presumptions. By bravely making a new decision, I found new roads and new horizons. Now, I believe I can build a new life, find a job abroad, and inspire people through my journey. I am very lucky to have met kind foreign friends who clarify my doubts and patiently teach me what I need.
I am an explorer, a dreamer, and a builder. My dreams drove me to explore new paths and build a new future. I aspire to be a UX designer, which demands deep knowledge, broad experiences, and belief that I can make the future better.
I am Louis Cheng. This is the start of my story.
